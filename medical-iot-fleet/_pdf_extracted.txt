Problem Statement
Hospitals rely on multiple critical medical devices for continuous patient care,
yet monitoring and managing these devices across different floors and rooms
remains challenging. Device failures, connectivity issues, or firmware errors
may go unnoticed until they disrupt operations, affecting efficiency and
potentially compromising patient safety.
In many healthcare settings, monitoring is periodic and manual, and
centralized lifecycle management is limited. There is a need for a system that
can continuously monitor device status, track usage, detect abnormal
behavior, and ensure timely firmware updates to maintain reliable and
efficient medical device operations.

===== PAGE 3 =====
Objectives
• Develop a centralized system to monitor and manage medical IoT devices
deployed across hospital environments.
• Continuously track device telemetry such as operational status,
connectivity, power levels, and sensor readings.
• Design an IoT-based communication system using ESP32 devices and
MQTT for real-time data transmission.
• Perform structured health checks to detect device faults, abnormal
behavior, or performance degradation.
• Provide alerts, usage tracking, and OTA firmware updates to improve device
reliability and lifecycle management.

===== PAGE 4 =====
Novelty
• Medical device monitoring in hospitals is often performed manually or through 
vendor specific systems.
• Many devices are monitored individually, and status checks are done 
periodically by technical staff.
• In several cases, faults or performance issues are identified only after a 
malfunction or downtime occurs.
• Existing enterprise IoT platforms provide fleet management, but they are often 
expensive, proprietary, and dependent on specific hardware ecosystems.
• Smaller healthcare institutions may lack centralized systems for real-time 
monitoring, usage auditing, and firmware management.
• Hence, there is a need for a customizable, centralized, and cost-effective system 
for continuous monitoring and intelligent management of medical device fleets.

===== PAGE 5 =====
Methodology
• Continuously monitor real-time device telemetry such as temperature,
pulse, or other sensor data from medical IoT devices.
• Track device operational status through periodic heartbeat signals to
verify whether devices are functioning properly.
• Collect and process sensor and status data using ESP32-based embedded
systems.
• Transmit device data wirelessly using MQTT protocol to a centralized
backend server.
• Analyze device performance using health scoring and anomaly detection
to identify abnormal behavior or potential failures.
• Provide alerts and notifications to administrators or medical staff when
devices go offline, exceed operational thresholds, or show warning-level
health scores.
• Support remote device control and Over-The-Air (OTA) firmware updates
for efficient lifecycle management.

===== PAGE 6 =====
Expected Outcomes
• Development of a functional prototype of a Medical IoT Device Fleet
Management System capable of monitoring the status and telemetry of
connected medical devices.
• A real-time monitoring platform that tracks device connectivity,
operational parameters, and sensor data through a centralized dashboard.
• Implementation of structured health checks to detect abnormal device
behavior, connectivity issues, or potential failures.
• Generation of alerts and logs to notify administrators about offline
devices, abnormal conditions, or maintenance requirements.
• A scalable and cost-effective prototype that demonstrates centralized
monitoring, OTA updates, and efficient lifecycle management of medical
IoT devices.

Scope of Proposal
• The proposed system enables centralized monitoring and management of
medical IoT devices deployed across hospital environments, improving visibility
of device status and operational reliability.
• The system can be further enhanced by integrating advanced analytics and
predictive maintenance techniques to identify potential device failures before
they occur.
• It can support long-term device data tracking, allowing administrators to
analyze performance trends, usage patterns, and maintenance history over
time.
• The solution has potential applications in hospitals, healthcare facilities, and
biomedical equipment management systems, supporting efficient device
lifecycle management and improved healthcare infrastructure monitoring.


===== PAGE 13 =====
Conclusion
• Medical device reliability is critical for effective hospital
operations and patient safety.
• Manual and periodic monitoring can delay the detection of device
faults or performance degradation.
• The proposed Medical IoT Device Fleet Management System
enables continuous real-time monitoring, centralized control, and
structured device management.
• By integrating MQTT communication, OTA firmware updates,
usage auditing and the system supports proactive maintenance
and intelligent supervision.
• This approach enhances operational efficiency, reduces
unexpected downtime, and demonstrates a scalable prototype for
modern medical IoT fleet management.
